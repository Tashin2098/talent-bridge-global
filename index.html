The current system

Hiring domestic help is still stuck in the 1990s.

Every step between an employer and a helper today runs through an agency that adds weeks, paperwork, and fees — without adding trust.

💸

Inflated fees

Agencies routinely charge employers and helpers a cut, sometimes equal to months of salary, just to make an introduction.

🐌

Slow, opaque process

Profiles get shared on PDF sheets. Calls get scheduled through a third person. Weeks pass before anyone actually talks.

🎲

Poor matching

Placements are often based on whoever is available, not who actually fits the household's needs, schedule, or expectations.

🔒

No direct relationship

Employers and helpers rarely speak before day one. Misaligned expectations surface only after someone has already moved.
